## Team Formation

The instructor will assemble project work teams, which will consist of a Project Manager (PM) and team members. The assigned PM will be the team leader and will be responsible for planning, organizing, directing, controlling, and coordinating the effort of the team to ensure that the assigned project and its deliverables are successfully completed by the established deadline.

Team sizes may vary based on course enrollment and the nature and scope of the projects to be completed; however, the total team will have no fewer than three (3) and no more than four (4). The goal is to keep team sizes small enough that each member will have an equal opportunity to make a substantive contribution to the overall success of the team in terms of meeting its goals and objectives.

> Important Notice: It is at the sole discretion of the instructor to change team members with or without advance notice, for cause or for no reason at all. In addition, the instructor has the right to change the parameters of the team projects, including but not limited to moving up or pushing back due dates, changing deliverable expectations, or requesting progress reports.

## Team Meetings (Units 2-7)

Each team will utilize the Canvas conferencing feature (Zoom) to conduct and record team meetings. Failure to record any of these mandatory meetings will adversely affect both the PM's and team members' grades, which will be assigned by the faculty at the end of Unit 7.

The meeting must be conducted at some point during the unit, which runs from Monday through Sunday. This allows flexibility to accommodate work schedules and the geographic location of the participants, whether domestic or international.

It is expected that a typical meeting will last 45 minutes to an hour depending on the level of group and individual preparation; however, there is no artificial limitation on the amount of time the team can participate in the meeting. Zoom permits the sharing of one's computer screen, which makes it possible to use these meetings as actual work sessions. There is no long-distance charge associated with accessing the Canvas conferencing feature.

## Project Description: Exploring Target Acquisitions

### Directions

Your team works for XYZ Company, which has a directional strategy focused on expanding the company through horizontal integration. Your team can determine the official name of the company and industry. The company does a great job keeping close watch on its cash position and consistently maintains a positive cash flow, is very solvent, controls its overhead expenses, has solid marketing and sales, production, and human resources performance metrics, and fosters a culture of strategic thinkers.

Historically, your company has expanded through a combination of organic (new startups) and inorganic growth and feels it is time to consider acquisition opportunities.

The Board is looking to engage in a friendly acquisition of a company that will not only increase its market share, but also allow it to penetrate new markets and increase the company's ability to meet current and future consumer needs and expectations. Since management's attitude is to pursue a friendly acquisition as opposed to a hostile takeover, your team may consider looking at conglomerates that have experienced significant growth through inorganic growth (acquisitions) and may now be looking to refocus on their core business and are willing to consider divesting some of their businesses that are within your industry. There could also be companies that are under financial duress and receptive to acquisition offers.

Your team is part of the corporate mergers and acquisition (M&A) department and has been assigned the task of identifying two potential acquisition targets. Since your Board is committed to a strategy of horizontal integration, you will be looking for possible acquisitions from within your industry. You are aware that the Board strategizes and supports green technology in companies that are guided by core values. You will be performing a preliminary analysis of the companies under consideration and then ultimately recommending one of the companies to move forward for a more in-depth valuation by the M&A Department.

> Reminder: You are working as a team member who will either lead or follow during this Team Project. Check out the resources provided to support your role during the project.
>
> Notes: The target acquisitions should be publicly traded and have the same fiscal year-end, preferably December 31. In addition, your team is encouraged to select a proper name for your company and the industry with which it is aligned.

## High-Level Process Flow

To successfully complete your preliminary analysis of the target acquisitions, your team should follow this high-level process flow.

### Step 1: Select Comparable Companies That Satisfy Inclusion Criteria

1. Describe the methodology used to select the target acquisitions. You may want to consider utilizing the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) to identify companies within your industry. There are also a variety of internet sites that can assist you in locating firms within your chosen industry, such as Google Finance and Yahoo Finance.

### Step 2: Conduct Qualitative Research on the Companies

1. Provide relevant background information on each company, current forces affecting its operations, and its expectations for future years. The Management Discussion and Analysis (MD&A) section of the company's annual report will be a useful source for this type of information.
2. In addition to the companies' annual reports, teams may pull current articles about the companies from databases available through the University Library. There are a number of credible sources where this type of information can be obtained through the internet.
3. Caution: It is dangerous to rely solely on the opinions and perceptions expressed by agents of a firm, since they may have a tendency to present an overly optimistic picture of the current state and future of their companies. It would be prudent to verify information through non-agents of the companies, such as financial and investment analysts who closely track the industry, as well as media outlets that closely monitor and report on companies within the U.S. economy. The objective is to validate the information you obtain through more than a single source.
4. Qualitatively analyze the content within the companies' most recent independent audit of their financials and records. In performing this analysis, focus on the audit findings and opinions.

### Step 3: Conduct Quantitative Analyses of the Companies

Financial ratio analysis and NPV.

1. Present a comprehensive financial statement analysis of the target acquisitions in some form of visualization. Prepare the common-size statements and all ratios and amounts for the measures given below for the most recent year for both companies, and present them in a clear tabular form. Then discuss each category.
2. Prepare common-size financial statements for both target acquisitions.
3. Complete both a horizontal and vertical analysis for each target acquisition.
4. Compare the common-size financial statements between the two companies using cross-sectional analysis.
5. Calculate and interpret relevant profitability, solvency, liquidity, leverage, and market value ratios for each company that will be useful in comparing the target acquisitions.
6. For each company, graphically trend net income and cash flow from operations over the last five years. Based on a comparison of the income statement to the statement of cash flows, explain which accounts create the greatest differences between net income (loss) and cash flow from operations. Comment on the quality of the earnings numbers.
7. Calculate and interpret the net present value (NPV) of the companies' annual free cash flow (FCF) for a 5-year period, taking into account a constant growth rate that must be calculated and a discount rate equal to your companies' weighted average cost of capital (WACC), which is 7%.

### Step 4: Prepare Report of Findings with Recommendation

1. Address any notable similarities and differences in the financial reporting practices of the two acquisition targets that could potentially impact your analysis. For example, the firms may use the same or different methods of accounting for property, plant, and equipment (PP&E), inventory, intangibles, and related items.

### Step 5: Construct Graphics or Analytics to Depict Your Recommendation

1. Include graphics or diagrams in your Final Deliverable Report that allow readers to visualize your recommendation. Choose analytics that clearly depict the story your acquisition recommendation is intended to tell.
2. You may use any software for this visualization, including Excel, Excel Goal Seek, Tableau, Google tools, Visio, or other software applications.
3. The team will perform all numeric calculations using Microsoft Excel. Excel can be used to graphically present data and then copied and pasted into the Report of Findings, or the material can be created directly in the Word document using Microsoft charting features.
4. For both blended students and online students, the spreadsheet must be uploaded along with the Report of Findings by Friday of Unit 8, no later than 11:59 p.m. (CT).

## Deliverables

### Unit 7: PowerPoint and Oral Presentation Description

There is no prescribed number of slides that must be contained within the slide deck. Specific criteria for the successful completion of the presentation, including both slides and oral delivery, are clearly laid out within the Presentation Grading Rubric. All participants are expected to dress in acceptable business attire and actively present content to the audience.

In the presentation, teams should present information that shows strategic initiatives will be followed with the acquisition. Teams should also ensure that corporate core values drive the decision to acquire the company.

Teams will deliver and record their presentation using the Canvas conferencing feature before a live, remote audience. The PM will need to determine a date and time that maximize audience participation. The Canvas recording of the presentation is to be archived by Sunday at 11:59 p.m. (CT).

For both blended students and online students, the PowerPoint slides are due by Sunday at 11:59 p.m. (CT) and must be uploaded under the appropriate Unit 7 assignment page. The PM is responsible for ensuring the slides are correctly uploaded by the deadline.

### Unit 8: Report of Findings with Recommendation

The team will prepare a Report of Findings that addresses, at a minimum, the following content: background information on the project, not the target acquisitions, as assigned to your team by the M&A Department of your company; the methodologies and assumptions used in selecting the target acquisitions; and the qualitative and quantitative analyses of company data. The report should present results with accurate interpretations, conclusions, and a recommendation regarding which company should move forward to the M&A Department for more in-depth valuation analysis (due diligence). The recommendation must be well-supported, properly vetted, and logically presented.

The team is expected to comply with APA (7th edition) standards for in-text citations and references; however, all other APA standards are suspended for purposes of this deliverable. Sound judgment and professional creativity are expected in the design, layout, and presentation of the report content. Graphs used to present financial data can be created in Microsoft Excel and copied into the report, or created directly in the Word document using Microsoft charting features.

There are no prescribed page limits associated with the report. Keep in mind that the team is expected to provide a great deal of specificity in how numeric calculations were performed, the underlying assumptions, and the interpretation of results. The content throughout the report must be substantive, thorough, and comprehensive; explicitly address the required components; reflect analysis, synthesis, and evaluation of relevant qualitative and quantitative data; and demonstrate quality consistent with that expected of an industry professional.

You are also required to prepare an Executive Summary to accompany the Report of Findings. The Executive Summary can be positioned at the beginning of the report for convenience, which is a common industry practice. Like the Report of Findings, the Executive Summary should demonstrate good judgment and professional creativity in design, layout, and content. Visualizations and data analytics may be created in Microsoft Excel, Tableau, Google tools, or other software for inclusion in the final deliverable. Graphics or other schematics may also be copied into your presentation to convey valuable information to management.

### Unit 8: Individually Written Paper on Decision-Making

Reflect on how decisions were made during the Team Project. Describe how you made decisions in a double-spaced paper of no more than four pages. Address the following:

1. Process for decision-making
2. Quantitative or qualitative nature of decisions
3. System 1-type or System 2-type thinking
4. Perception thinking (convergent or divergent)
5. Benefits of collaboration
6. Theory for thinking, such as reflective, integrative, system, perception-based, values-based, or design thinking
7. How you will strive to make decisions in the future
